{% comment %} Yelp Redesign Quality of life changes and incentives to increase user traffic Problem Statement How might we increase the number of monthly reviews given on the Yelp desktop app? Overview I and two other group members were assigned with redesigning Yelp's desktop app page in order to increase the number of monthly reviews on its platform, and how we can achieve this. We focused on adding elements that would improve the quality of life for users, as well as an original feature that will incentivize users to continue using their account Team Group Project consisting of 3 members (including myself) Programs Used Figma, Photoshop, Whimsical Brainstorm and Research Interviews: 6 Indirect and 5 Ethnographic (Usability) Key Takeaways: Users either too lazy or not compelled enough to write reviews Users only consider reviewing based off a really positive or negative experience. Would be more willing to review if provided incentives Users typically use yelp to explore new options based on reviews and attached photos​​​​​​​ User Personas, Use Case, Journey Map User Persona Use Case Hungry wants to find a new restaurant through Yelp. She decides on a restaurant but finds it difficult to navigate the surplus of info. She finally reaches the reviews, but she is having trouble with recommendations due to a lack of buzzwords and reviews with images. Hungry has a good time, but does not leave a review because it was uneventful and not incentivized to do so. She closes Yelp and forgets about it until the next time she wants to eat out. Pain Points: No incentive, backtracking is not easy, crowded pages with no tabs Journey Map Proposed Solution: Concise Feed & Incentives Consolidating the business page with drop-down menus to prevent needless scrolling and a shortcut to the reviews section through a scrolling tab on the right. Furthermore, a brand new feature: Yelp Rewards. Businesses can partner with Yelp by offering promos when users leave reviews on their page Flow Chart Wireframes Homepage Redesigned homepage to direct attention to "first review" button, which was originally hardly noticeable. Emphasized button in hopes of encouraging new users to write their first review Quick links redirect to recommended/trending restaurants in local area in hopes of increasing recurring users Business Page Original business page had abundance of relevant information, but not all were necessary to show at once. Dropdown menus will tidy of page to prevent needless scrolling while condensing all information Number of reviews next to business name is now clickable (underlined) and will send user to reviews tab. Original page does NOT scroll user to reviews. Review highlights and "review" button displayed on right side of page to provide user with relevant reviews throughout page Deals and Rewards Page New original page for incentivizing users to review Features best deals at local restaurants and other businesses Tab that shows what you earn by reviewing and other stats such as lifetime savings Mock-Ups Homepage "Write your first review!" button implemented Uniform image dimensions and a larger emphasis on places/deals/businesses around you Business Page Dropdown tabs for specific topics, easy to navigate for what you want to look for Sidebar contains additional images and keywords to increase ease of usability for users Recommended food item featured in menu tab alongside pictures for a more authentic look of the items Deals and Rewards + Claim Demo Ticket stub icons with corresponding symbols and nice colored gradients Filter rewards with buttons up above Upon finishing review, thank you screen to confirm completion as well as a ticket stub reward Figma Prototype Please set to fit width under options for best user experience! Reflection It was a privilege working with two amazing group members who also put in their absolute best in order to create a satisfactory redesign. As my first project that followed the UX lifecycle, I was somewhat surprised at the amount of brainstorming and researched needed prior to actually designing the site. I initially was confused why the project length was a whole week, because I thought designing pages with group members would speed the time up much more, but I never factored in research, brainstorming, collaborating with members for additional input, etc. There was a lot of first times for me in this project, but I learned a lot about reasons behind motivations for people. {% endcomment %}
